Richard Kauli is a designer and fabricator from Brooklyn, NY whose work explores freedom, surveillance, and physicality in the digital age. Raised in a family that encouraged building and observation, he’s always been drawn to making things by hand.

His fashion label, FREE, merges technical skill with philosophical storytelling, often combining digital fabrication with manual craft. He’s showcased work across multiple continents and continues to build tools and garments that question the systems we live in.

Outside of fashion, Richard experiments with materials like wood and metal, creating functional sculptures that blur the line between product and art. His mission is to make people think through form, texture, and purpose.

His journey started with photography and urban exploration, eventually shifting to garment making after a personal experience revealed how meaningful and expressive clothing could be.

With each collection, he aims to merge conceptual thought with craftsmanship, asking viewers to question not only what they wear but why they wear it.
